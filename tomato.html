Pepper Breeding at Agridera

Agridera strives to be a global frontrunner in the breeding, production, and marketing of high-quality pepper seeds. Our mission is to combine intense heat with exceptional agricultural performance, providing growers across diverse climates with reliable and productive hybrids.

Currently, our primary focus is on integrating robust resistance packages into our elite breeding lines.We prioritize resistance to Pepper Mild Mottle Virus (PMMoV), Potato Virus Y (PVY), and Bacterial Leaf Spot (Xanthomonas). Furthermore, our R&D team is actively working on varieties with enhanced tolerance to Phytophthora capsici and other soil-borne pathogens, ensuring stable yields even under challenging environmental conditions.

In our breeding programs, we place heavy emphasis on a wide range of horticultural traits. We select for optimal Scoville heat levels (pungency) concentrated fruit set, and uniform fruit ripening. Key characteristics include deep red or vibrant yellow fruit color, thick fruit walls for better shelf life, and high adaptability to extreme temperature fluctuations. We offer a diverse portfolio of shapes and types, ranging from classic Cayenne and Jalapeño profiles to Susi and elongated Grenlan varieties, all engineered for maximum market value.

Agridera Pepper breeding programs are focusing on three main segments: Greenhouse Indeterminate Peppers, Open-field Indeterminate Peppers, and Bush Type Open-field Peppers.

Greenhouse Pepper Breeding Program

This breeding is aimed for the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern markets, specifically focusing on high-end greenhouse production. We select for uniform fruit shape, consistent pungency (Scoville levels), and essential resistances including PMMoV (Pepper Mild Mottle Virus), TSWV, and Nematodes.Some hybrids are specifically developed to include additional resistances like Powdery Mildew (Leveillula taurica) to ensure high performance in protected environments.

Open-field Indeterminate Peppers

This breeding is designed for the Mexican, United States, and Southeast Asian markets. The main focus is on high-yielding varieties with large, high-quality fruits (Cayenne and Serrano types); and resistances to PVY (Potato Virus Y), TSWV, and Bacterial Leaf Spot (Xanthomonas) In the near future, we aim to integrate resistance to Phytophthora capsici as a standard feature in our indeterminate open-field cultivars to help growers manage soil-borne diseases.

Open-field Bush Type Peppers

This breeding program targets the global industrial and processing markets. We breed for a variety of shapes—from classic elongated chilies to blocky Jalapeño types—focusing on concentrated fruit set for mechanical harvesting. We combinePMMoV, PVY, and TEV (Tobacco Etch Virus)as basic resistances in our hybrids. Additionally, certain hybrids include traits for improved shelf life and superior color retention after drying or processing.

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
